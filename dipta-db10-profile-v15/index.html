The Player

Maharddhika Daniswara Budiman, known as Dipta, is a midfield intelligence built through layered exposure rather than a single environment. He is most naturally a central player, equally credible protecting deeper midfield space, linking attacking combinations, covering the right-back channel or stretching the game as a right winger.

His value is not flash. It is clarity, technical security and decision-making under pressure. For any team that prizes intelligent movement, he is precisely the kind of profile that becomes harder to sign with every season he keeps progressing.

The Journey

Built layer by layer, since age three.

The story of how a young midfielder learned to understand the game from many angles.

Dipta started early. In 2018, at only three years old, he joined Banana Kicks in Jakarta, where football first became a structured part of his daily growth. In 2019 he widened his grassroots experience with Buffalo FC, before the Covid-19 pandemic briefly paused youth football.

From 2022 he developed at Brazilian Soccer Schools Indonesia, through the Senayan and Menteng environments. This was the technical core of his game: ball mastery, creativity, dribbling confidence and small-sided decision-making. He was named Best Player at the BSS Holiday Camp 2023, joined the BSS Regional Team in 2025, and stayed connected to BSS until early 2026.

In 2024 he was invited to the Royal Indonesia Football Academy, associated with the Real Madrid Foundation methodology, validating his central-midfield profile. In 2025 he was selected to the STY FC Academy elite team and the ASIOP FC Academy semi-elite and elite team, raising his training standards and tactical demands.

His pathway is best read as simultaneous, not sequential. As of 2026 he is active across RIFA, STY, ASIOP and Garuda Muda Indonesia at once, gaining breadth of coaching, competition and tactical references without losing continuity.

His international exposure keeps growing. In 2025 he played KFAM Kuala Lumpur with STY and the Milo Challenger Cup Kuala Lumpur with the BSS Regional Team. In 2026 he was called up by Garuda Muda Indonesia for the MAXIM Cup KL and listed for the Piala Dunia Anak Indonesia.

He also leads. As captain at Global Sevilla Pulomas he guided his school team to repeated success and was twice named Best Player of the Tournament. His next growth areas are natural for his profile: physical presence, progressive risk-taking, end-product consistency and left-foot execution. The foundation is already visible.

He can connect, cover, create and compete. A player learning to understand the game from different angles, and one worth monitoring closely. On Dipta's profile

Measurement Reference

INSAIT JOY is a wearable-based performance tracking system used in training and matches. It records movement and athletic output across a session and aggregates the data into Personal Best and Personal Average values for each parameter.

Read each bar as a comparison between Dipta's own peak output (Personal Best) and his own typical output (Personal Average). Both figures are Dipta's, his best recorded session against his average, not a ranking against other players. Distance and high-speed figures reflect work-rate and repeatability, while speed, acceleration and jump reflect explosiveness.
